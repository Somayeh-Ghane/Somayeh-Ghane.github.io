About

I hold an MSc degree in Biodiversity Management & Conservation with research experience in molecular phylogenetics, phylogeography, population genetics, and conservation genetics. My work integrates field sampling with hands-on laboratory workflows (DNA extraction, PCR, gel electrophoresis) and downstream analytical pipelines to support evidence-based conservation and biogeographic inference.

Phylogeography mtDNA markers DNA extraction & PCR Geneious Prime BEAST / MrBayes / RAxML ArcGIS

Featured Research (Priority): 2021 Global Ecology and Conservation

In our 2021 study, we investigated how the Mid-Pleistocene Transition may have shaped diversification patterns in the Irano-Anatolian region using the eastern three-lined lizard as a model system. The research combined broad-scale sampling, multi-gene mitochondrial datasets, and phylogeographic inference.
